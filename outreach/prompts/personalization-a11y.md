# Ultra Zoom personalization prompt — accessibility bucket

You are writing a single opening sentence for a cold email about **Ultra Zoom**, a privacy-focused browser zoom extension by Lost Rabbit Digital.

The recipient writes for an accessibility, low-vision, or assistive-technology publication. Tone matters more here than in any other bucket: lead with utility, never with pity, never with savior language. People with low vision are experts in their own tooling. The opener does not pitch — it just shows you read the post and respect the audience.

The recipient recently published this post:

- **Title:** {title}
- **Domain:** {domain}
- **Description / excerpt:** {description}

Write ONE opening sentence that:

1. References something **specific and concrete** from the post — a tool they reviewed, a barrier they discussed, a WCAG point they made, a personal workflow they shared, a piece of assistive tech they evaluated. Generic praise about accessibility as a cause is not allowed.
2. Reads like someone in the field noticed the post in passing, not a marketer following a template.
3. Sounds natural as the first line of an email that will (in the next sentence the human writes) suggest Ultra Zoom be considered for a future roundup or update.

Hard constraints:

- **Maximum 25 words.**
- No em dashes (`—`). Use commas, periods, or parentheses if you need a pause.
- No sycophantic openers: do not begin with "I loved", "great post", "amazing article", "this is fantastic", "what a wonderful", or any variant.
- No pity language: do not use "suffering from", "afflicted", "struggle with", "disability community" as a generic.
- Do not use the words "stumbled" or "amazing".
- Do not put quotes around the sentence.
- Do not introduce yourself, do not mention Ultra Zoom, do not pitch anything.
- Output **only** the sentence. No preamble, no explanation, no quotes, no signature.
