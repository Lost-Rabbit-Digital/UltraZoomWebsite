# Ultra Zoom B2B opener prompt

You are writing the first 1–2 sentences of a cold email to a decision-maker at a company whose staff routinely review lots of detailed photos, scans, screenshots, or imagery in a browser as part of their day. The product is **Ultra Zoom** by Lost Rabbit Digital — a privacy-focused browser zoom extension. Free install, $4/mo per seat for the pro features (position memory, per-site presets, smooth pan, hotkeys). Pitch (which the human writes after these two sentences): "let your team try the pro version free for a couple of weeks, then decide".

The recipient was found via an Apollo people-search filter that targeted image-heavy B2B roles — auto/property/medical claims adjusters, real-estate appraisers, manufacturing QA inspectors, auction-house catalogers, GIS analysts, radiology / pathology operations leads, genealogy/archive directors, and so on.

The recipient:

- **Name:** {first_name}
- **Title / role:** {title}
- **Company:** {company}
- **Niche / why we found them:** {niche}
- **Profile or company summary:** {summary}

Write **exactly two sentences**, plain text, no markdown:

1. **First sentence**: a concrete, specific observation about what their team's work involves — anchored in the niche, title, and company summary. Examples of good anchors: "auto adjusters comparing collision photos against vehicle build sheets", "property inspectors flagging hairline foundation cracks from drone shots", "QA staff catching defects on PCB scans before reflow", "appraisers reading hallmarks off jewelry photos", "catalogers identifying coin die varieties from supplier scans".
2. **Second sentence**: name the visual-detail pain that comes with that work — squinting at thumbnails, ctrl-+ zooming and losing position, screenshot-and-magnify, opening images in a new tab to actually see the detail, repeating the same zoom-pan gesture across hundreds of images a day. This sets up the Ultra Zoom pitch.

Hard constraints:

- **Two sentences total. 50 words maximum across both.**
- No em dashes (`—` or `–`). Use hyphens, commas, or periods.
- Do not name Ultra Zoom or Lost Rabbit Digital. Do not pitch.
- Do not begin with "I", "Hi", "Hello", "Hope", "Just", "Hey". Start with a noun referencing the team's work or a verb phrase describing it.
- No flattery: "love your work", "impressive", "incredible", "passionate", "amazing".
- No marketing words: leverage, synergy, transform, empower, world-class.
- No emojis. No quotes around the output.
- Output **only** the two sentences. No preamble, no explanation, no signature.
