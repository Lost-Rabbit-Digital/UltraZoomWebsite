# Ultra Zoom power-user opener prompt

You are writing the first 1–2 sentences of a cold email to a power-user prospect for **Ultra Zoom**, a privacy-focused browser zoom extension by Lost Rabbit Digital. Free install, $4/mo upgrade for advanced features (position memory, per-site presets, smooth pan, hotkeys).

The recipient was found via LinkedIn people-search. The pitch (which the human writes after these two sentences) is: "this is the cleanest pixel-level zoom workflow for what you do — try the free version, $4/mo for the pro features."

The recipient:

- **Name:** {first_name}
- **Title / role:** {title}
- **Niche / why we found them:** {niche}
- **Profile summary:** {summary}

Write **exactly two sentences**, plain text, no markdown:

1. **First sentence**: a concrete, specific observation about what their work involves — anchored in their title, niche, or a detail from the profile summary. Examples of good anchors: "appraising vintage cameras from auction listings", "tracing 1890 census handwriting", "inspecting coin die varieties from supplier scans".
2. **Second sentence**: name the visual-detail pain that comes with that work — squinting at thumbnails, ctrl-+ zooming and losing position, screenshot-and-magnify, opening images in a new tab to actually see the detail. This sets up the Ultra Zoom pitch.

Hard constraints:

- **Two sentences total. 50 words maximum across both.**
- No em dashes (`—` or `–`). Use hyphens, commas, or periods.
- Do not name Ultra Zoom or Lost Rabbit Digital. Do not pitch.
- Do not begin with "I", "Hi", "Hello", "Hope", "Just", "Hey". Start with a noun referencing their work or a verb phrase describing it.
- No flattery: "love your work", "impressive", "incredible", "passionate", "amazing".
- No marketing words: leverage, synergy, transform, empower, world-class.
- No emojis. No quotes around the output.
- Output **only** the two sentences. No preamble, no explanation, no signature.
