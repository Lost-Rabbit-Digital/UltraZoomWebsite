# Ultra Zoom Realtors — Touch 1 reference

This is the cold-open touch. The recipient has never heard from us. Subject
and body must read like one realtor (David, founder of Ultra Zoom)
emailing another, not like a sales sequence.

## Reference subject

> Hairline crack on Zillow, or shadow?

The subject doesn't have to be that exact line. Vary the question or the
specific defect (water stain, paint chip, baseboard damage, lighting
glare on hardwood) so a recipient receiving this in proximity to another
agent gets a different opener. **Hard cap: 9 words or fewer.** Frame it
as a concrete on-the-job problem, not a pitch. Short fragments and
sentence-cased questions both work; clickbait and all-caps do not.

## Reference body

```
Hey {{first_name}},

Quick one. How often do you click into MLS or Zillow photos
just to check if that's a crack, water stain, or shadow?

Built Ultra Zoom for this. Hover over any listing photo and
it expands to full detail without clicking through. Works on
Zillow, Redfin, Realtor.com, and major MLSs.

20-second demo: {{landing_page_link}}

If it's worth a look, REALTOR30 gets you 30% off your first year.

David
Ultra Zoom
```

## Structure beats

A complete Touch 1 body hits these in order:

1. Greeting on a single line: `Hey {{first_name}},`
2. One-question hook tied to a specific photo-review pain (1-2 sentences)
3. One-paragraph value prop: name the product, what it does, where it
   works (Zillow / Redfin / Realtor.com / major MLSs)
4. Demo link line — the literal merge tag `{{landing_page_link}}` on its
   own line or end of a sentence
5. Coupon line — `REALTOR30` referenced verbatim with the offer phrasing
6. Signoff: `David` then `Ultra Zoom` on the next line

## Personalization room

You may adjust:
- The opening question to reference the lead's city, brokerage style,
  or a vertical signal from the keywords list (luxury, foreclosures,
  rentals, commercial vs residential)
- The phrasing of the value prop to match the lead's apparent client
  mix (urban condo agent vs rural land specialist need slightly
  different framings)
- The subject line within the constraints above

You may NOT:
- Replace `{{landing_page_link}}` with a real URL
- Replace `REALTOR30` with another coupon or remove it
- Add a P.S., a calendar link, or any other CTA the reference doesn't have
- Use em-dashes (always a period or comma instead)
- Reference the lead's name in the body more than once after the greeting

## Tone

Short sentences. Period more than comma. First-person from David. No
adjectives that aren't load-bearing. The reader is busy, mid-tour, or
glancing at this on a phone between appointments.
