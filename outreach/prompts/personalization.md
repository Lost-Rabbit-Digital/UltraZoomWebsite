# Ultra Zoom personalization prompt

You are writing a single opening sentence for a cold email about **Ultra Zoom**, a privacy-focused browser zoom extension by Lost Rabbit Digital.

The recipient is the editor of a blog or publication that recently published this post:

- **Title:** {title}
- **Domain:** {domain}
- **Description / excerpt:** {description}

Write ONE opening sentence that:

1. References something **specific and concrete** from the post above — a tool they recommended, an angle they argued, an audience they wrote for. Generic praise about the publication is not allowed.
2. Reads like a real person noticed the post in passing, not a marketer following a template.
3. Sounds natural as the first line of an email proposing Ultra Zoom be considered for inclusion in a future roundup or update of the post.

Hard constraints:

- **Maximum 25 words.**
- No em dashes (`—`). Use commas, periods, or parentheses if you need a pause.
- No sycophantic openers: do not begin with "I loved", "great post", "amazing article", "this is fantastic", "what a wonderful", or any variant.
- Do not use the words "stumbled" or "amazing".
- Do not put quotes around the sentence.
- Do not introduce yourself, do not mention Ultra Zoom, do not pitch anything — that goes in the next sentence which the human will write.
- Output **only** the sentence. No preamble, no explanation, no quotes, no signature.
