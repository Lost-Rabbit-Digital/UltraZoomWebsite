# Ultra Zoom personalization prompt — genealogy bucket

You are writing a single opening sentence for a cold email about **Ultra Zoom**, a privacy-focused browser zoom extension by Lost Rabbit Digital.

The recipient writes for a genealogy or family-history publication. Their daily readers spend hours squinting at low-resolution scans: census records, faded newspapers, handwritten ledgers, probate files, ship manifests. Browser zoom hits a pixelation wall well before the detail they need is visible. Ultra Zoom solves that, but the *opener* does not pitch — it just shows you read the post.

The recipient recently published this post:

- **Title:** {title}
- **Domain:** {domain}
- **Description / excerpt:** {description}

Write ONE opening sentence that:

1. References something **specific and concrete** from the post — a record set they discussed (e.g. 1880 census, FamilySearch wills), an archive they used, a research method, a surname brick wall, a digitization project. Generic praise about genealogy as a hobby is not allowed.
2. Reads like a fellow researcher noticed the post in passing, not a marketer following a template.
3. Sounds natural as the first line of an email that will (in the next sentence the human writes) suggest Ultra Zoom be considered for a future tools roundup or how-to update.

Hard constraints:

- **Maximum 25 words.**
- No em dashes (`—`). Use commas, periods, or parentheses if you need a pause.
- No sycophantic openers: do not begin with "I loved", "great post", "amazing article", "this is fantastic", "what a wonderful", or any variant.
- Do not use the words "stumbled" or "amazing".
- Do not put quotes around the sentence.
- Do not introduce yourself, do not mention Ultra Zoom, do not pitch anything.
- Output **only** the sentence. No preamble, no explanation, no quotes, no signature.
