This is the 5-day follow-up to a Touch 1 that got no reply. MailMeteor
will thread it under the original subject, so this email lands inside
the same Gmail conversation as Touch 1. Body should be much shorter
than Touch 1 — three short paragraphs at most.

## Reference subject

> Re: How long does it take you to spot a hairline crack on Zillow?

The subject MUST start with `Re: ` followed by the same question used in
Touch 1's subject. MailMeteor uses subject matching for threading. If
Touch 1's subject was personalized to the lead's city or specialty, the
T2 subject mirrors it with `Re: ` prepended. (Operator note: the
runner enforces the `Re: ` prefix automatically; you do not have to add
it yourself in the JSON, but keep the rest of the subject aligned with
T1's voice.)

## Structure beats

1. Greeting: `Hey {{first_name}},`
2. One-line nudge: "closing the loop / circling back / one last check"
   framed as a yes/no choice
3. One-line out: explicitly give the recipient permission to ignore. The
   no-pressure tone is the entire point of T2.
4. Coupon nod: `REALTOR30` mentioned once. No new link — the recipient
   already saw it in T1.
5. Signoff: `David` on its own line. No company name on T2 (T1 already
   established it).

## Personalization room

You may adjust:
- The exact phrasing of the nudge (vary "closing the loop" / "circling
  back" / "one more check" so the second touch doesn't read identically
  across the batch)
- A tiny reference to a specific signal from the lead (one phrase, e.g.
  "for your buyer-agent flow" or "for the Phoenix listings"), but keep
  T2 shorter than T1 — this is a closer, not another pitch.

You may NOT:
- Add a new link or new CTA
- Re-pitch the product features (T1 already did)
- Apologize for following up
- Use em-dashes (period or comma)
- Exceed 80 words total in the body

## Tone

Even shorter than Touch 1. Conversational, low-pressure, friendly. The
recipient should feel that ignoring this email is a real option David is
genuinely fine with.
